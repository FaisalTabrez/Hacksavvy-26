About The Event

Join us at HackSavvy, exclusively hosted by the Mahatma Gandhi Institute of Technology, for a premier national-level 24-hour hackathon dedicated to students passionate about advancing the frontiers of innovation. Immerse yourself in the realm of technology, exploring a wide range of themes including Artificial Intelligence (AI), Cybersecurity, Internet of Things (IoT), Machine Learning, Blockchain Technology, Drone Technology, Robotics, and Sustainable Infrastructure. This national event provides a unique platform for students from across the country to engage in deep collaboration, guided by industry experts, to turn innovative ideas into practical solutions. HackSavvy is the ideal stage for students to challenge the status quo, enhance their technical prowess, and achieve significant breakthroughs. Circle the date for an extraordinary experience of learning, growth, and the exhilaration of making a tangible difference through technological innovation at a national scale.

Where: MGIT, Hyderabad

When: Thursday – Friday
12th – 13th Feb 2026

About MGIT

Knowledge is Power

Mahatma Gandhi Institute of Technology (MGIT) has experienced rapid growth since its establishment in 1997 by the Chaitanya Bharathi Educational Society (CBES) in the serene and tranquil surroundings of Gandipet, Hyderabad.

MGIT is an autonomous engineering institute accredited by NAAC with A++ grade and approved by AICTE, with several NBA-accredited programs. It is consistently ranked among notable engineering colleges and offers strong placement outcomes with good industry connections.

In its two decades and more, all the stakeholders of the Institute, relentlessly endeavoured to position MGIT as a Leader and an Innovator in the ecosystem of technical education. The Institute has established excellent Infrastructure such as state-of-the-art laboratories, spacious library with a collection of printed and digital books & journals, sports & hostel facilities along with Infrastructure for extra and co-curricular engagements in pursuit of academic excellence. MGIT has scaled greater heights both Nationally & Internationally and made its mark in Industry and in academia.

Guidelines for The Event

Team Formation:

Each team should consist of 2-5 members.We encourage collaboration and diversity in skill sets for a well-rounded team.

Registration Fee:

A non-refundable fee of Rs. 2500 per team is required for regill be provided upon registration.

Eligibility:

This event is open to all college students.A valid college ID and a government-issued ID are mandatory for verification at the event.

Project Development:

All development work must be done during the hackathon hours.Pre-existing projects or cases of plagiarism will lead to disqualification.

Equipment:

Participants are required to bring their laptops, chargers, and any necessary hardware.Internet access will be provided at the venue.

Submission:

Teams must submit their project by the end of the hackathon period for evaluation.

Judging Criteria:

Projects will be evaluated based on creativity, technical difficulty, impact, and presentation. To know more about the judging criteria, visit the Judging Criteria section.

Awards and Recognition:

Certificates will be awarded to all participants.Special prizes will be given to top-performing teams.

Food and Refreshments:

Meals (Breakfast, lunch, and dinner) will be provided to keep participants energized.Snacks and beverages, including tea and coffee, will also be available.

Before You Begin:

Please ensure you have the following ready for a smooth registration process: names, email addresses, college IDs, etc.

Proof of payment for the registration fee (Rs. 2500 per team).

FAQ's

Who can participate?

This event is open to all undergraduate and postgraduate college students. A valid college ID and a government-issued ID are mandatory for verification at the event.

When will the applications close?

Applications are accepted on a first-come, first-served basis due to limited seats. Registration will close once we reach our capacity (the registration form will become unresponsive).

How does the application process work?

For a detailed explanation of the application process, please view our schedule.

How much does it cost?

The participation fee is Rs 2500 per team.

Can we apply as a team?

Yes, teams can have a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 5 members. You are also welcome to form teams with students from other colleges. Note: If two teams with the same problem statement receive equal marks, priority will be given to the team with fewer members.

What if I don't have a team or an idea?

For guidance on forming a team or generating ideas, please refer to our guidelines.

Do you provide accommodation for participants?

Yes, we provide limited accommodation on a first-come, first-served basis, prioritizing those who email us first. Accommodation includes food, snacks, tea/coffee, and a nap area.

Can I change my theme after registration?

Yes, but you need to send an email to hacksavvy@mgit.ac.in before registration closes.

Can you provide travel assistance (TA) for students?

No, we do not provide travel assistance.

Judging Criteria

With a grand prize pool of ₹2,50,000 Lakhs, HackSavvy-26 sets a high bar for excellence. Projects will be evaluated by an esteemed panel of judges based on innovation, technical implementation, problem relevance, scalability, and overall impact. Teams are encouraged to present solutions that are not only creative but also practical, user-centric, and sustainable in real-world scenarios.

Innovation and Creativity: Judges will assess the originality of theidea and the creativity demonstrated in solving the problem.

Technical Complexity: The technical depth and sophistication of the solution will be evaluated, including the use of advanced technologies and coding practices.

Impact and Feasibility: The potential impact of the project on users and its feasibility for real-world application will be considered.

User Experience and Design: The usability, design aesthetics, and overall user experience of the project will be judged.


Presentation and Communication: Participants will be evaluated on their ability to effectively communicate their ideas, demonstrate their project, and answer questions from judges.

Three Prizes will be awarded to the top teams in each domain based on the judging criteria mentioned above.

PS-1.1: City-Scale AI-Driven Urban Risk Intelligence System ▾

Problem Statement

Modern urban environments face compound and cascading risks such as flooding, heatwaves, and air pollution due to climate change, rapid urbanization, and aging infrastructure.

Participants are required to design an AI-driven early warning and decision-support system that integrates real-time IoT data, satellite imagery, historical climate data, and urban infrastructure information.

Objectives

• Predict and monitor urban flooding, heatwaves, and air pollution events

• Fuse heterogeneous real-time and historical data sources

• Provide explainable insights for policymakers

• Support scenario-based policy simulations

Expected Deliverables

• AI/ML models for risk prediction

• Interactive decision-support dashboard

• Explainability and uncertainty visualization

• System architecture and technical documentation

Problem Statement

Healthcare AI solutions often fail in resource-constrained hospitals due to data silos, lack of interpretability, and fairness concerns. There is a need for a clinically reliable and explainable AI system that can integrate diverse medical data while adhering to ethical and regulatory requirements.

Participants must design a multi-modal diagnostic AI framework integrating medical images, EHRs, lab reports, and wearable data.

Objectives

• Enable accurate multi-modal medical diagnosis

• Ensure model explainability and trust

• Detect and mitigate demographic bias

• Enable deployment in low-resource settings

Expected Deliverables

• Multi-modal AI diagnostic model

• Explainability module (visual/textual)

• Bias and fairness evaluation report

• Deployment feasibility analysis

• Demonstration prototype

Cyber Security & Blockchain

PS-2.1: Advanced AI-Based Phishing and Social Engineering Detection Platform ▾

Problem Statement

Phishing and social engineering attacks have evolved beyond simple spam emails to highly targeted, context-aware attacks exploiting human psychology. Existing detection systems often fail to identify sophisticated threats such as Business Email Compromise (BEC), credential harvesting, and impersonation-based scams.

Design and develop an AI-powered, multi-modal phishing detection platform capable of analyzing email content, sender metadata, embedded URLs, webpage structures, and user behavior patterns to detect and alert users about sophisticated phishing and social engineering attacks in real time.

Objectives

• NLP-based intent detection (urgency, fear, authority cues)

• URL lexical analysis, reputation scoring, and webpage similarity detection

• Sender behavior profiling (domain age, sending frequency, anomalies)

• Explainable AI to justify detection decisions

• Integration as a browser plugin with backend microservices

Expected Deliverables

• Functional prototype (plugin + backend)

• Detection accuracy evaluation

• Explainability reports

• Technical documentation and demo video

PS-4.1: Intelligent AI-Powered Rural Microgrid System ▾

Problem Statement

Rural and remote regions across India continue to face unreliable, intermittent, or inadequate access to electricity. Hybrid microgrids integrating renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, biomass, and energy storage provide a sustainable solution; however, effective operation is challenged by intermittent generation, variable demand patterns, affordability constraints, and limited infrastructure.

Design and develop an AI-powered rural microgrid management system that intelligently optimizes hybrid energy sources, predicts rural energy demand, and ensures reliable, affordable, and sustainable electricity access for rural communities.

Objectives

• Optimize the utilization of multiple renewable energy sources

• Forecast rural energy demand under uncertain usage patterns

• Improve reliability and reduce power outages

• Ensure affordability and energy equity

• Support scalable deployment in low-infrastructure environments

Expected Deliverables

• AI-based energy demand forecasting module

• Hybrid microgrid optimization and control logic

• Simulation or prototype of microgrid operation

• Reliability and affordability impact analysis

• Technical documentation and demonstration

The rapid integration of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind has introduced significant uncertainty into power grid operations. Variability in weather patterns, fluctuating demand, and limited storage capacity pose challenges to grid stability, energy reliability, and carbon reduction goals. Traditional forecasting and control mechanisms are inadequate to handle these complexities in real time.

Design and develop an AI-driven renewable energy forecasting and grid optimization system that integrates weather data, renewable generation data, and energy storage information to improve grid stability, optimize energy utilization, and minimize carbon emissions.
